# Design Discovery Brief — {Product Name}

> NO BRIEF = NO DESIGN. This template is the gate function. Every section must be filled before any visual work begins. If a section is blank, the design will be generic. Generic = death.

## 1. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

- **Market tier:** Enterprise / Mid-market / Mass consumer?
- **Demographics:** Age range, income bracket, technical literacy level?
- **Context of use:** When/where/how do they encounter this product? (Desktop at work? Phone on commute? Tablet at home?)
- **What do they currently use?** Name specific products, not categories.
- **Why is their current solution failing them?** What's the breaking point that makes them search for alternatives?

## 2. Primary Goal

- **What is the ONE action the landing page optimizes for?** (Sign up / Book demo / Purchase / Join waitlist / Download)
- **What is the conversion mechanism?** (Email only? Email + password? Credit card? Calendar booking?)
- **What friction exists between landing and conversion?** (Trust deficit? Price uncertainty? Feature confusion? Too many steps?)
- **What does the user need to BELIEVE before they convert?** (This is safe / This saves me money / This is better than what I have)

## 3. Emotional Tone

- **What should the user FEEL within 3 seconds of landing?** (One sentence, visceral)
- **Word pairs describing desired emotional response:** (e.g., "calm confidence" not "excitement", "professional trust" not "fun")
- **What emotions must be AVOIDED?** (e.g., urgency/pressure for a finance tool, playfulness for a medical product)
- **Real-world analogy:** "Using this product should feel like \_\_\_" (e.g., "hiring a great accountant", "walking into a well-organized store")

## 4. Audience Problem

- **What specific pain point does this product solve?** (Be concrete, not abstract)
- **How much does this pain cost them?** (In dollars, hours/week, or stress level)
- **How do THEY describe this problem in their own words?** (Not marketing speak — their actual language)
- **What's the moment of peak frustration?** (The trigger that makes them Google for a solution)
- **What have they already tried that didn't work?** (Failed solutions = trust barriers to address)

## 5. Competitive Context

- **Top 3 competitors by name:**
- **What do competitors do WELL visually?** (Steal this — don't reinvent what works)
- **What do competitors do POORLY visually?** (Differentiate here — this is your opening)
- **What visual cliches exist in this vertical?** (Avoid these — they signal "generic")
- **What would make someone choose THIS over the incumbent?** (The visual must reinforce this differentiator)

## 6. Success Metrics

- **Primary KPI:** (conversion rate, signups, demo bookings — ONE number)
- **Target for primary KPI:** (specific number, e.g., "8% landing-to-signup")
- **Secondary KPIs:** (time on page, scroll depth, return visits)
- **What does "working" look like after 30 days?** (Concrete outcome, not vague improvement)
- **What does FAILURE look like?** (Know this so you can design against it)
